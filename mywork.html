Michael Jordan: The GOAT Case

Chicago Bulls Era (1984-1993, 1995-1998)

During his tenure with the Bulls, Jordan defined basketball excellence:

  1. The First Three-Peat
    • Defeated Magic Johnson and the Lakers in 1991
    • Averaged 31.2 PPG over the 1992 season
    • Won 3 consecutive Finals MVPs
  2. The Return and Second Three-Peat
    • Led the 72-10 season in 1995-96
    • Won the "Flu Game" in the 1997 Finals
    • Hit 'The Last Shot' in 1998

Career Accolades & Stats

Jordan's legacy is backed by unmatched numbers:

  • 6-0 Finals record (Never lost a Finals series)
  • 5-time NBA League MVP
  • 10-time NBA Scoring Champion
  • 1988 Defensive Player of the Year
  • Career average of 30.1 PPG (Highest in NBA history)
